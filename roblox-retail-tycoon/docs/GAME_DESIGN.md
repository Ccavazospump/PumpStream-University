# Retail Tycoon — Game Design Document

A Roblox tycoon game where you run your own grocery/retail store, inspired by
how a real supermarket (like H-E-B) operates — customers, departments,
personal shoppers, and checkout — turned into a fun gameplay loop.

## The Fantasy

You start with an empty storefront and four crates of produce. Customers walk
in and order at the counter, and **you** are the personal shopper: run to the
shelf, grab the item, run back, hand it over, ring them up. As money comes in
you unlock real store departments and eventually hire staff to do the running
for you — going from solo shopkeeper to store manager.

## Core Loop (moment-to-moment)

1. A customer NPC walks in from the street and stops at the counter.
2. A speech bubble above them shows their order (e.g. `Apple 0/2, Milk 0/1`).
3. You run to the right department, hold **E** at a shelf to grab an item
   (it stacks visibly above your head).
4. Run back and **Hand Over Items** at the customer. Repeat until the order
   shows all ✅.
5. **Checkout** — cash register sound, money in your pocket, customer leaves
   happy. Sometimes they tip!
6. If you're too slow (2 minutes), they storm out angry and you earn nothing.

## Progression Loop (session-to-session)

Cash → upgrades → more/faster/bigger orders → more cash. Progress (cash +
upgrades) saves between play sessions.

### Departments (unlock in order)

| Department | Cost | Items | Avg. item value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Produce | free | Apple, Banana, Carrot, Tomato | ~$5 |
| Dairy | $500 | Milk, Cheese, Eggs, Yogurt | ~$12 |
| Bakery | $1,500 | Bread, Donut, Croissant, Cake | ~$19 |
| Cosmetics | $4,000 | Soap, Shampoo, Lipstick, Perfume | ~$31 |

More departments = bigger orders (up to 4 different items) and higher-value
items, so income scales up as the store grows.

### Upgrades

| Upgrade | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Shopping Basket | $250 | Carry 3 items at once |
| Marketing I / II | $400 / $1,600 | +2 customers in store each |
| Fast Service I / II | $700 / $2,200 | Customers arrive sooner |
| Tip Jar | $900 | +25% checkout pay |
| Shopping Cart | $1,800 | Carry 6 items at once |
| **Hire Personal Shopper** | $2,500 | NPC fetches ordered items for you |
| **Hire Cashier** | $3,500 | NPC rings up completed orders |

With both staff hired the store runs itself and the player graduates to
managing/expanding — the classic tycoon "automation" payoff.

## World Layout

- 6 claimable plots arranged along a street; step on the glowing pad to claim.
- Each plot is a 90×60 store: door and checkout counter at the front,
  four department zones (color-coded floor + hanging sign + item shelves),
  and a row of upgrade pads in the front-right corner.
- Everything is generated by code at runtime — no manual building required,
  and layout changes are just number tweaks in `PlotBuilder.lua`.

## Balancing Notes

- Starting cash $100; a produce order pays ~$10–25, so the first upgrade
  (Basket, $250) arrives within a few minutes.
- All prices/costs/timers live in `src/shared/GameConfig.lua` — tune the whole
  economy from one file.

## Future Ideas (post-MVP roadmap)

- **Curbside/online orders** — a printer spits out a pickup order with a
  timer; the true H-E-B personal-shopper experience.
- **Restocking** — shelves hold limited stock; a delivery truck arrives at a
  back dock and you (or a Stocker employee) restock shelves.
- **Rebuilding/decor** — floor tiles, shelf skins, store name customization.
- **Rush hour events** — double customers for 2 minutes, big payout.
- **Leaderboards** — richest store, most customers served.
- **Gamepasses** — VIP customers, auto-collect, 2x cash (monetization).
- Replace the blocky NPCs with proper R15 rigs + walk animations.
