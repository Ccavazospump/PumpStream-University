# Retail Tycoon — Game Design Document

A Roblox tycoon game where you run your own grocery/retail store, inspired by
how a real supermarket (like H-E-B) operates — customers, departments,
personal shoppers, and checkout — turned into a fun gameplay loop.

## The Fantasy

Run a real supermarket the way a real one works. Walk-in customers shop the
aisles **themselves** and line up at your register — you ring them up. Then
you unlock **Online Orders**, and the personal-shopper gameplay kicks in:
orders ping in, you race the aisles gathering them, and hand them off at
curbside spots outside for premium pay. Hire a Cashier to run the register
and Personal Shoppers to work curbside, and graduate from clerk to manager.

## Core Loops (moment-to-moment)

**Walk-ins (the register loop):**
1. A customer walks in, browses the aisles, and visibly collects their own
   groceries (you can watch their stack grow).
2. They line up at the register showing 💰 "Ready to check out!"
3. Stand at the register and use **Checkout Customer** — ka-ching, money in
   pocket, sometimes a tip. Too slow (90s) and they storm out unpaid.

**Curbside (the personal-shopper loop — unlocked with Online Orders, $1,500):**
1. 📱 *Ding!* — an alert: a new online order, with a customer heading to a
   curbside spot outside.
2. Their order floats over the spot. You run the aisles, hold **E** to grab
   items (X puts back mistakes; the Returns Bin dumps everything).
3. Hand the order over at the curbside spot: **1.5× item value + $10 fee.**
4. Buy more curbside spots to run multiple orders at once — or hire
   Personal Shoppers to work them for you.

## Progression Loop (session-to-session)

Cash → upgrades → more/faster/bigger orders → more cash. Progress (cash +
upgrades) saves between play sessions.

### Unlock chains (tycoon-style: one pad at a time)

Three chains run in parallel; each buy-pad only appears once the previous
upgrade in its chain is owned.

**Store chain** — departments + expansions (pads sit on the floor of the
area they unlock):

| Unlock | Cost | Items |
|---|---|---|
| Produce | free | Apple, Banana, Carrot, Tomato, Watermelon, Grapes |
| Dry Goods Aisle | $400 | Pasta, Cereal, Rice, Canned Soup, Peanut Butter, Chips |
| Dairy Aisle | $1,000 | Milk, Cheese, Eggs, Yogurt, Butter, Cream |
| Bakery | $1,800 | Bread, Donut, Croissant, Cake, Bagel, Muffin |
| **Store Expansion I** | $2,600 | pushes the back wall out |
| Frozen Aisle | $3,400 | Ice Cream, Frozen Pizza, Fries, Ice Pops, Veggies, Fish Sticks |
| Meat & Deli | $4,600 | Chicken, Steak, Bacon, Sausage, Ham, Turkey |
| **Store Expansion II** | $6,500 | widens the whole store (side wings) |
| Cosmetics Wing | $7,500 | Soap, Shampoo, Lipstick, Perfume, Lotion, Toothpaste |
| Household Wing | $9,500 | Detergent, Paper Towels, Sponges, Bleach, Trash Bags, Air Freshener |

**Register chain** (one pad spot near the door): Basket $250 → Marketing I
$500 → Fast Service I $800 → Tip Jar $1,200 → Cart $2,200 → Marketing II
$2,800 → Fast Service II $3,600 → Pro Shopper Bag $5,200 → Golden Tip Jar
$7,000.

**Staff chain** (second pad spot near the door): Personal Shopper $2,800 →
Cashier $4,000 → 2nd Personal Shopper $6,800.

With staff hired the store runs itself and the player graduates to
managing/expanding — the classic tycoon "automation" payoff.

## World Layout

- 6 claimable plots arranged along a street; step on the glowing pad to claim.
- Each store starts small (70×50) and expands twice, ending at 110×80 with
  8 aisle-style departments (two facing rows of shelves each), a front-left
  checkout counter, and a Returns Bin for putting back wrong items
  (or press **X** to put back the last item).
- Customers are real R15 characters with walk animations; products are
  little multi-part models (not solid cubes) built procedurally in
  `ItemVisuals.lua`.
- Everything is generated by code at runtime — no manual building required,
  and layout changes are just number tweaks in `PlotBuilder.lua`.

## Balancing Notes

- Starting cash $100; a produce order pays ~$10–25, so the first upgrade
  (Basket, $250) arrives within a few minutes.
- All prices/costs/timers live in `src/shared/GameConfig.lua` — tune the whole
  economy from one file.

## Future Ideas (post-MVP roadmap)

- **Curbside/online orders** — a printer spits out a pickup order with a
  timer; the true H-E-B personal-shopper experience.
- **Restocking** — shelves hold limited stock; a delivery truck arrives at a
  back dock and you (or a Stocker employee) restock shelves.
- **Rebuilding/decor** — floor tiles, shelf skins, store name customization.
- **Rush hour events** — double customers for 2 minutes, big payout.
- **Leaderboards** — richest store, most customers served.
- **Gamepasses** — VIP customers, auto-collect, 2x cash (monetization).
- Replace the blocky NPCs with proper R15 rigs + walk animations.
